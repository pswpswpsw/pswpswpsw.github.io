Bio
I am a Tenure-Track Assistant Professor in the Department of Mechanical, Aerospace, and Nuclear Engineering at The Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute, Troy, NY 12180 in the United States. I lead the Computational Scientific Machine Learning Lab. I am also affiliated with Rensselaer-IBM Artificial Intelligence Research Collaboration. Prior to that, I was a Postdoc in the AI Institute in Dynamic Systems at the University of Washington, Seattle collaborated with Nathan Kutz and Steve Brunton. I obtained my Ph.D. in Aerospace Engineering and Scientific Computing in 2021 from the University of Michigan, Ann Arbor, advised by Karthik Duraisamy.
Our Computational Scientific Machine Learning (CSML) Lab is dedicated to transformative computational and data-driven approaches to the predictive forward/inverse modeling, and control of complex large-scale nonlinear dynamic systems (e.g., fluid flows), with the ultimate goal of accelerating scientific discovery.
Our computing resources include computational awards on supercomputing clusters: RPI-AiMOS, Purdue Anvil, NERSC Perlmutter, TAMU Faster, NREL Kestral, New York State Empire-AI Beta system, and a private workstation of 3xA6000 48GB GPUs. Besides, each Ph.D. student is equipped with a high-end workstation with a 3090 24GB GPU.
